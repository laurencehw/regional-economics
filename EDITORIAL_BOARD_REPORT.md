#### Chapter 3A: Spatial Econometrics
**AI-isms flagged:** "sheds light on" (once)

**Rewritten opening:**
> When a factory opens in one county, wages rise in the next. When a city invests in transit infrastructure, land values shift three jurisdictions away. Conventional econometrics treats each observation as independent — an assumption that fails the moment geography enters the model. This chapter develops the toolkit for estimation when outcomes are spatially correlated: spatial weight matrices, tests for autocorrelation, and the SAR/SEM/SDM model family that resolves the bias OLS inherits from ignoring spillovers.
